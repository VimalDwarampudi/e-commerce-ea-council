# Risk Management & Compliance Principles

## RC-PRIN-001: Risk Visibility Over Risk Avoidance

The goal is not to avoid all risk — it is to make risk visible, owned, and managed. Hidden risk is the most dangerous kind. Surface it, quantify it, and assign an owner.

---

## RC-PRIN-002: Compliance is a Floor, Not a Ceiling

Meeting regulatory requirements is the minimum. Architecture should aspire to better practice, not stop at the compliance line.

---

## RC-PRIN-003: Evidence Over Assertion

Compliance claims must be backed by evidence: logs, test results, assessments, certificates. "We think we comply" is not a compliance posture.

---

## RC-PRIN-004: Risk Ownership Is Business Ownership

Risk is owned by the business, not by IT or the architecture council. The council identifies and quantifies risk. The business decides what level of risk to accept. Architecture implements the agreed controls.

---

## RC-PRIN-005: Prevention Is Cheaper Than Remediation

Compliance is cheaper to build in than retrofit. A GDPR violation costs more than a privacy-by-design review. Include compliance requirements in design, not in post-launch incident response.

---

## RC-PRIN-006: Proportionality

The depth of risk and compliance assessment must be proportional to the impact and sensitivity of the proposal. A Minor change in an internal tool warrants a different level of rigor than a Major change to a customer-facing payment system.

---

## RC-PRIN-007: Know Your Regulatory Landscape

The organization must maintain awareness of applicable regulations across all jurisdictions where it operates. Ignorance of a regulation is not a legal defense.
